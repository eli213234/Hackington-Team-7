Mandarin is a group of Chinese languages in the language family Sino-Tibetan. Mandarin is native to China, Taiwan, Singapore, and other Chinese communities, primarily in northern and southwestern China and is the official language of People’s Republic of China and Taiwan. This language has around 918 million speakers making up 12% of the world's population.

Spanish is a Romance language in the Indo – European language group, originating in the Iberian Peninsula. This language due to the Spanish Empire spread to Central America, South America, and the Caribbeans. Its enormous colonial expansion took it not only to America but also to Africa and Asia. Due to migration, the United States is the country with the second-largest number of Spanish speakers in the world.

  • Native speakers: 460 million

French is an Indo – European language of the romance subgroup. It originated from Vulgar Latin like all other romance languages. It is spoken primarily in France, Belgium, parts of Canada, and many countries in western and central Africa such as the Congo. This language has 77.2 million speakers making up 1% of the world's population.
